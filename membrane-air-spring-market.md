<p><p>The membrane air spring market is divided into two main types: self-sealing and non-self-sealing. Self-sealing air springs automatically maintain air pressure, ensuring consistent performance and reducing the need for manual maintenance. They are ideal for applications requiring reliability and low maintenance. In contrast, non-self-sealing air springs require regular attention to maintain air pressure, as they rely on external support systems. This type is often used in applications where cost-efficiency takes precedence over automatic maintenance features.</p></p>
