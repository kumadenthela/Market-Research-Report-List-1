<p><p>The coronary artery imaging market encompasses various imaging modalities, each serving distinct diagnostic purposes. MRI equipment provides detailed soft tissue images without radiation, while CT scanners offer rapid, high-resolution 3D images of coronary arteries. X-ray equipment is used for basic assessments, particularly in angiography. Single Photon Emission Computed Tomography (SPECT) provides functional imaging, measuring blood flow and metabolism. Positron Emission Tomography (PET) offers insights into cellular activity, while PET/CT combines metabolic and anatomical data for comprehensive evaluations of coronary health.</p></p>
