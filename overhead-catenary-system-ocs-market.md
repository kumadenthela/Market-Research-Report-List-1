<p><p>The Overhead Catenary System (OCS) is a crucial infrastructure for electrified rail transport, supplying power to trains via suspended wires. The market is categorized into three types: Low Voltage OCS, typically used for trams and light rail, delivers up to 1 kV; Medium Voltage OCS, ranging from 1 kV to 15 kV, is suitable for suburban and regional trains; High Voltage OCS, exceeding 15 kV, powers high-speed and heavy freight trains, supporting extensive rail networks globally.</p></p>
