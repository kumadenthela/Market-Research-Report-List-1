<p><p>Isothermal Nucleic Acid Amplification techniques enhance DNA and RNA analysis without temperature cycling. Key types include LAMP (Loop-mediated Isothermal Amplification), known for its rapid and robust amplification at a constant temperature, making it suitable for point-of-care diagnostics. HDA (Helicase-Dependent Amplification) utilizes helicases to separate DNA strands, allowing for efficient amplification. Other methods may include techniques like RPA (Recombinase Polymerase Amplification) and NASBA (Nucleic Acid Sequence-Based Amplification), each with specific advantages for sensitivity, speed, and operational simplicity in nucleic acid testing.</p></p>
